Case Study 01 · UX Design · Mobile

Digital First & Mobile Personalisation of Servicing Pages

Led a mobile-first and digitisation redesign of servicing pages to improve customer experience and retention across help and support journeys.

Retention 72% → 87% Mobile-first

Problem

Users were experiencing friction when navigating servicing pages on mobile, leading to drop-offs and lower engagement. Users weren't seeing the right steps to complete their chosen task on desktop and tablet.

Role

Product Designer (UX & Content)

Process

  • Analysed user behaviour and engagement data
  • Identified key friction points in mobile journeys
  • Designed a mobile-first template highlighting the steps customers will take on their mobiles first
  • Designed a desktop and tablet template highlighting the steps customers will take in this platform first
  • Will be introducing deep-linking to app to further improve the mobile experiences for seamless continuous journey

Key Decisions

  • Simplified page hierarchy highlighting which steps come first depending on the device the customers will use
  • Focused on reducing interaction steps
  • Clear CTAs to continue journey
  • Enclosed extra support in accordions to reduce page length

Outcome

Retention increased from 72% to 87%. Improved usability across key journeys and created reusable templates for future pages and different squads to use.

Reflection

A big takeaway here was how small structural changes, especially on mobile, can have a huge impact on retention.

Case Study 02 · UX/UI Design · Mobile App

Bumblebees — Social Networking App for Mothers

Bumblebees is a mobile social networking app designed to connect mothers, reduce isolation, and provide accessible postnatal support. The project was completed as part of the Love Circular UX/UI Bootcamp using a Human-Centred Design methodology.

Problem

The COVID-19 pandemic significantly disrupted postnatal care, leaving new mothers isolated and undersupported. Existing apps offered guides and information but none provided peer-to-peer matching — a critical gap this product was designed to fill.

Role

Lead UX/UI Designer | Figma, InVision Freehand

Process

Led the full design lifecycle from research through to high-fidelity prototyping. This included competitor analysis across three direct competitors, qualitative user interviews with pandemic-era mothers, affinity mapping, user journey mapping, and a feature prioritisation matrix. Research revealed that mental wellbeing, financial awareness, and life management were the highest-priority user needs — informing every design decision that followed.

Outcomes

Delivered low, mid, and high-fidelity wireframes alongside a fully prototyped onboarding flow in Figma. The design incorporated a matchmaking feature, personalised content journey, educational resources, and an accessible style guide tested against WCAG colour contrast standards. UI laws applied included the Aesthetic Usability Effect, Law of Common Region, Jacob's Law, and Hick's Law.

Case Study 03 · UX Design · Journey Mapping

MBNA Pay by Debit Card — Mobile App Journey Redesign

An end-to-end UX audit and redesign of the MBNA mobile app journey for paying a credit card using a debit card. The project mapped the existing 15-screen flow, identified friction points across every stage, and proposed a streamlined 12/13-screen journey to improve usability and customer confidence.

Problem

The existing payment journey contained multiple points of confusion, redundant screens, and unclear navigation — particularly around payment source selection, amount input, and the handoff to third-party processor Fiserve. Users experienced frustration, loss of confidence, and disorientation at critical decision points, undermining trust in the payment process.

Role

UX Designer | Lloyds Banking Group | Journey Mapping & Service Design

Process

Created a detailed end-to-end experience map covering user actions, digital UI screenshots, emotional states, pain points, and proposed solutions at every stage of the journey — from MBNA app homescreen through to payment confirmation and return. Key issues identified included an overwhelming homepage, unclear payment type selection, redundant screens, an unrecognisable Fiserve handoff with no brand continuity, poorly labelled input fields, and a confusing exit flow back to the MBNA app.

Outcomes

Delivered a proposed revised user journey reducing the flow from 15 to 12/13 screens by eliminating redundant steps, pre-selecting known account details, and improving content hierarchy throughout. Recommendations included cleaner homepage personalisation, revised screen titles, compressed confirmation details, full account number display, and a clearer return-to-app action — directly addressing the identified drop-off and confusion points.
